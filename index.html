Arcade Silent Film

The only thing louder than the lights is the drama. This is chaos, turned silent.

What happens when overstimulation gets the silent treatment? Arcade Silent Film reimagines the loudest room in the building — an arcade — as a scene straight out of the 1920s. It’s a contradiction in motion. Pixel flashes and power-ups clash with intertitles and grainy drama, all without a single sound. This piece leans into irony, nostalgia, and visual noise to explore how mood and movement can speak louder than audio. It’s a love letter to silent films, glitchy visuals, and the kind of cinematic tension that doesn’t need a soundtrack.

Filmed in a local arcade. Edited using Adobe Premiere Pro and After Effects. Styled with a 1920s silent film aesthetic including black and white grading, intertitles, jump cuts, dramatic pacing, and layered grain overlays.
